Legal aspects
What you need to protect yourself
🚨 URGENT — Equity agreement in writing
Before you write a single line of code get your equity percentage confirmed in writing via email at minimum. Even just "I confirm Morolake receives X% of Urban HelpHub revenue" replied by your friend is legally meaningful. Your dad can help draft something more formal. This protects you if anything goes wrong later.
🚨 URGENT — Terms and conditions on the platform
Before launch the platform needs Terms of Service and Privacy Policy pages. These protect you legally if a job goes wrong, a user gets scammed, or a dispute happens. Use a free generator like termsfeed.com to create these — your dad can review them.
Register the business in Alberta
Once you start making real money register as a business through Alberta Business Registry (alberta.ca). Options: Sole proprietorship (~$60), Partnership (~$60), or Corporation (~$450). Your dad as a business analyst can advise which is best for your situation. Don't do this until you have users and revenue.
GST registration
In Canada you must register for GST once you earn over $30,000/year. Under that threshold it's optional. Keep this in mind as the platform grows. Your dad or an accountant can help with this when the time comes.
Data privacy — PIPEDA compliance
Canada's privacy law (PIPEDA) requires you to protect user data, have a clear privacy policy, and not sell user information. Firebase and Oracle both have strong security — just make sure you have a privacy policy page before launch.
Payment liability
Stripe handles payment security and PCI compliance — you're not responsible for storing card numbers. However your Terms of Service should clearly state the platform is not responsible for disputes between posters and workers. Add a dispute resolution process.
Worker classification
Workers on your platform are independent contractors, not employees. Make this clear in your Terms of Service. They are responsible for their own taxes on earnings. This is how Uber, TaskRabbit and Fiverr all operate.
Insurance consideration
Eventually consider platform liability insurance — in case something goes wrong during a job (e.g. worker damages property). Not needed at launch but worth thinking about at 500+ users. Your dad can advise on this.
